Analytics Capstone Project — Data Analytics Tool

For my capstone project, we worked with a large video game company to streamline the player id retrieval process such that players can be invited for upcoming playtests.

  • The turnaround time for Project Managers from requesting to receiving player IDs from the analytics team can take between 1-2 weeks
  • It is time-consuming for Data Analysts to understand the specific player demographics that the Research Project Managers are looking to invite
  • Project Managers cannot directly access player data themselves due to a variety of reasons (i.e. player privacy data)
    • How might we improve this?
  • Project Managers, Research Team, the Analytics team
  • Reduce turnaround time for player ID retrievals
  • Reduce the amount of time spent for this type of reoccuring task
  • Create an efficient solution that can streamline this process

    • team of 4, consisting of a Data Analyst, Project Manager, Business Analyst, and me

    My roles: UX testing, User Research, and Product Management

    Received very positive feedback and enthusiasm from Project Managers for this project, as well as with the analytics team we worked directly with

    Imagine This

    You are a Research Project Manager responsible for managing a playtest/user research session with the goal of collecting feedback about an upcoming feature. Your objective is to understand how players precisely feel about the feature, and the most effective way to achieve this is by reaching out to current players interested in beta testing unreleased features. The current process involves obtaining a list of playerIDs, which they insert into another tool which will then email players if they may be interested in trying this up and coming feature, while also being compensated for their time.

    However, the only way you can access the list of playerIDs is by contacting the analytics team and scheduling a meeting with them to describe your demographics so that they can execute an SQL query on the database to access this information.

    For the analytics team, they provide support for over 20 other game titles, each of which has its own Research Project Managers responsible for conducting their playtest and user research sessions. Currently, writing SQL queries to generate playerIDs is considered a necessary, albeit less preferred, reocurring task for the analytics team in the current working process for the company. The team would prefer to spend their resources on generating impactful insights from data.

    How might we:

  • Reduce the turnaround time it takes for Project Managers to get PlayerIDs?
  • Reduce the amount of workload from the analytics team from doing this task?
  • Ensure data compliance?

    • 1. Research Methodology & Solution Development

    We worked closely with 4 Project Managers and 2 members from the analytics team when developing a prototype design. Our development process proceeded as follows:

  • Exploratory Interviews with Project Managers to understand the specific use cases
  • Lo-fi Mockup
  • Internal testing and critique with analytics team
  • Hi-fi Prototype Design v1
  • Internal testing and critique with analytics team round 2
  • Hi-fi Prototype Design v2
  • User Testing with Research Project Managers
  • Hi-fi Prototype Design v3

    • 2. Personas

    From working with our stakeholders, we identified two primary personas that are directly affected by this inefficiency. As a group, we decided that the best way forward was to develop a self-service interface for project managers to use.

    3. Top 3 Research Findings

    1. Most playerid data requests can be loosely summarized as 70% basic, 20% intermediate, and 10% advanced complexity of demographics. Project Managers have estimated that this self-service tool can cover a majority of the basic and intermediate requests (between 70-90% of all data requests).

  • Basic requests tend to contain common variables and inclusions. I.e.
    (Game="Some_Game_Name" AND Country="United States" AND GameTime>500hours AND Level>100)
  • Intermediate queries tend to include many exclusions and OR states
    (Game = 'Some_Game_Name' AND Country = 'Canada' AND GameTime > 500 AND Level > 100) or
    (Game = 'Some_Game_Name' AND Country = 'United States' AND City NOT IN ('NewYork', 'Dallas', 'Chicago', 'NewOrleans', 'Boston') AND GameTime > 500 AND Level > 100);

    • 2. There are deadlines and time constraints for when a playtest/user research session occurs. So, Research Project Managers may selectively decide and request for a less-than-ideal player demographic because they know what to expect from it. The risk of generating a bad-sample size can be catastrophic due to turnaround times.

    3. Most playerID data requests are very basic and can be specified within the following parameters of Game, Language, Location, Playtime, and some unique variables (e.g., game modes).

    • 6. Reflections & Takeaways

    If we had a few more months to work on this, I am confident that we could have translated the designs into code, and built a fully functional product. With the solution we proposed, we expect to reduce turnaround times, save an estimated $72,000 annually, and give Project Managers more flexibility in selecting player demographics.
