VAS — Building the Community

At VAS, I supported the project management to build an all-inclusive customer help site.

At the time, VAS employed more than 20 staff members to provide customer support. The volume of customer calls appeared to be on a constant rise, especially as the company expanded into new regions. This necessitated the creation of a comprehensive help website.

  • Farm operators and farm workers
  • Maintain the delivery pace of Jira Tickets
  • To shift customers from calling to utilizing the VAS Community

    • team of 6 Developers, 2 Content Writers, 2 UX Designers, 1 Product Owner, and 1 project coordinator (me)

    My roles: Project Management, Marketing

    27% Adoption rate of customer base within first 2 months.

    The Project

    The VAS Community was designed to serve as an all-inclusive help website where customers can access articles, engage in discussions with other farmers on forums, and seek customer support through live chats. As time passed, the objective was to encourage customers to use this service more frequently than contacting the customer support, as it proved to be a more cost-effective option.

    Daniel's Role:

  • Daniel supported the project management within a 2 week sprint environment and facilitated team discussions for stand-up meetings (3x/week), sprint reviews, and retrospectives.
  • Responsible for forecasting Sprint iterations with the development team and stakeholders. He managed the queue of deliverables, adjusting priorities according to the needs of the business on the sprint board within the project management software, Jira.
  • Worked with the Product Owner to investigate the list of Jira tickets within the backlog
  • Crafted the Launch and engagement strategy project to invite the existing customer base onto the customer forum/support site. This consisted of working closely with Marketing and managing deliverables, personally creating and managing the A/B test invitation email campaigns, and executed upon an engagement strategy to keep users engaged for the first 2 months of the launch. i.e. Weekly discussions, giveaways, etc.
